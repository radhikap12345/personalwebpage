Hello! I am Radhika.

I am a PhD student in ME department at Stanford since Fall 2016. My research focuses on experimental characterization of mechanical properties of nanomaterials. I synthesize nanoparticles, thin films and nanostructures and study their strengths, deformation mechanisms and other relevant mechanical properties. I work with Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) and Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM) and wonder of times I had read about them in high school books.

I love products, designing and innovation. That is why I picked Mechanical Engineering as major. I also like to learn new computer technologies to think about innovating smart products.

I am a highly audio-visual person very fond of colors and music. In my free time I love to paint or hum and sway to guitar and keyboard. Over time I have turned into a health freak paying close attention to running, swimming, gymming and healthy eating habits. And I love social dancing, something I am very proud I picked up at Stanford. Waltz, polka and swing are some of my favorites, and one in this crowd is me :).

Happy to get in touch, converse, discuss and learn about anything.
